\section{Context}

\subsection{Curiosity Origin}
As travelers, we are often curious about how people move through large cities.
In particular, we wanted to understand mobility patterns, the choices people make when navigating urban spaces, and the factors that influence those choices.
\\
This curiosity led us to explore data sources that could provide meaningful insights into urban movement.
Among them, New York City's bike rental data stood out as a rich source of information on how people travel across the city.
When combined with other datasets, it can offer a clearer picture of the city's daily dynamics.
\\
We also learned that this bike rental data is provided by a private company (nome) that operates the bike-sharing service in New York City.
For this reason, making our analysis useful and relevant to the company is an effective way to increase its practical impact.

\subsection{Problem Statement}
In this context we defined the following requirements and restrictions:

\subsubsection*{Target Audience}
Our project is mainly designed for the bike rental company, as they can use the insights from our analysis to optimize their services and improve customer satisfaction.
From a business perspective, understanding mobility patterns can help the company make informed decisions about bike placement, pricing strategies, and marketing campaigns.
\\
However, the findings can also be valuable for city planners, but also for the general public.
For city planners, the insights can inform infrastructure development and urban planning decisions.
For the general public, the visualizations can raise awareness about mobility patterns and encourage more sustainable and efficient transportation choices.

\subsubsection*{Deliverables and Comsumption}
To support the easy and effective consumption of our insights, we chose to develop an interactive dashboard.
This allows all types of users to explore the data and insights in a user-friendly and immediate way.
In this way, we can include in a compact way a variety of visualizations and insights, making it easier to filter and explore the analysis.
\\
Regarding the frequency, we expect our dashboard to be automatically updated on a regular basis, since the data are continuously collected and updated.
This ensures that the insights can be useful and relevant over time, permitting also history analysis and comparisons between different time periods.
\\
Our idea for the setting was to offer an immediate insights to be the more impactful at first view, but for to also to permit a deeper exploration of the data and insights for users who want to dive into the details.

\subsubsection*{Resources}
Since we are working with a large dataset and we want to create an interactive dashboard, we need to have programming skills and experience with programming tools.
\\
Basically, we expect to use Python for data retrieval, cleaning, and analysis, and JavaScript (with libraries like D3.js) for the interactive visualizations in the dashboard.
This choice is motivated by the fact that Python is a powerful and widely used language for data analysis, while JavaScript is essential for creating interactive web-based visualizations.

\subsection{Purpose}
We expect to identify the following information and insights from our analysis, and to make them available to our target audience through the dashboard:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Heatmaps of station
    \item Daily/weekly/monthly usage patterns
    \item User membership analysis
    \item Trip duration and distance analysis
    \item Correlation with weather data
\end{itemize}
Our objective is to provide an mainly informative dashboard in both a fast and detailed way.
\\
The visualization is designed to provide an exploratory experience. 
The dashboard allows users to interact with the data and investigate different aspects of bike-sharing usage.
Since we are providing a variety of visualizations and insights, users can explore the data from different angles and derive their own conclusions.
This approach encourages users to derive their own insights from the dataset.
