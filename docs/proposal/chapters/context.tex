\section{Context}

\subsection{Curiosity Origin}
As travelers, we are often curious about how people move through large cities.
In particular, we wanted to understand mobility patterns, the choices people make when navigating urban spaces, and the factors that shape those choices.
\\
This curiosity led us to explore data sources that could provide meaningful insights into urban movement.
Among them, New York City's bike rental data stood out as a rich source of information on how people travel across the city.
When combined with other datasets, it can offer a clearer picture of the city's daily dynamics.
\\
We also learned that this bike rental data is provided by a private company (nome) that operates the bike-sharing service in New York City.
For this reason, making our analysis useful and relevant to the company is an effective way to increase its practical impact.

\subsection{Problem Statement}
In this context we defined the following requirements and restrictions:

\subsubsection*{Target Audience}
Our project is mainly designed for the bike rental company, as they can use the insights from our analysis to optimize their services and improve customer satisfaction.
From a business perspective, understanding mobility patterns can help the company make informed decisions about bike placement, pricing strategies, and marketing campaigns.
\\
However, the findings can also be valuable for city planners and the general public.
For city planners, the insights can inform infrastructure development and urban planning decisions.
For the general public, the visualizations can raise awareness about mobility patterns and encourage more sustainable and efficient transportation choices.

\subsubsection*{Deliverables and Consumption}
To support the easy and effective consumption of our insights, we chose to develop an interactive dashboard.
This allows all types of users to explore the data and insights in a user-friendly and immediate way.
In this way, we can present a wide range of visualizations and insights in a compact format, making the analysis easier to filter and explore.
\\
Regarding update frequency, we expect the dashboard to be refreshed automatically on a regular basis, since the data are continuously collected.
This ensures that the insights remain useful and relevant over time, while also enabling historical analysis and comparisons across different periods.
\\
Our design approach is to present clear, immediate insights at first impactful while also allowing deeper exploration for users who want to examine the details.

\subsubsection*{Resources}
Since we are working with a large dataset and aim to create an interactive dashboard, we need programming skills and experience with data and visualization tools.
\\
We expect to use Python for data retrieval, cleaning, and analysis, and JavaScript (with libraries such as D3.js) for interactive visualizations in the dashboard.
This choice is motivated by the fact that Python is a powerful and widely used language for data analysis, while JavaScript is essential for creating interactive web-based visualizations.

\subsection{Purpose, Vision and Tone}
Our objective is to help users understand mobility patterns in New York City in a clear and informative way.
\\
We aim to provide an \textbf{exploratory} tool that allows users to interactively discover insights and patterns in the data.
Users should be able to highlight and filter categories on their own.
\\
This result is particularly suitable for the bike rental company, since they have domain knowledge of both the business and the data.
By providing them with this tool, they can extract insights that are relevant to their operations and make informed decisions based on the data.
\\
However, we also aim to give the general public the opportunity to explore the data by making the insights accessible and understandable to a wider audience.
This can help raise awareness of mobility patterns and encourage more sustainable transportation choices.
\\\\
The tone we want should be clear and easy to follow, with slight engagement to maintain user interest in exploring the data and insights.
At the same time, the data should remain the main focus, so we aim to avoid distracting or manipulative presentation choices.
